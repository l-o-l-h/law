* RCW 4.08.080 Action on assigned choses in action

- Any assignee or assignees
  - of any
    - judgment,
    - bond,
    - specialty,
    - book account, or
    - other chose in action,
    - for the payment of money,
  - by assignment in writing,
    - signed by the person authorized to make the same,
- may,
  - by virtue of such assignment,
- sue and
- maintain
  - an action or
  - actions
  - in his or her name,
  - against
    - the obligor or
    - obligors,
    - debtor or
    - debtors,
    - named in such
      - judgment,
      - bond,
      - specialty,
      - book account, or
      - other chose in action,
- notwithstanding
  - the assignor
  - may have an interest in the thing assigned:
- PROVIDED,
  - That
    - any debtor
    - may plead
    - in defense
    - as many
      - defenses,
      - counterclaims and
      - offsets,
    - whether they be such as have heretofore been denominated
      - legal or
      - equitable, or
      - both,
    - if
      - held by him
        - against the original owner,
        - against the debt assigned,
      - save that
        - no counterclaim or
        - offset
        - shall be pleaded
        - against
          - negotiable paper assigned before due, and
          - where the holder thereof
            - has purchased the same
              - in good faith and
              - for value, and
          - is
            - the owner of all interest therein.

* Action on an Assigned Account

In an action on an assigned  account, where defendant sets up defenses
seeking equitable relief against  assignor, court may require assignor
to  be made  party to  the end  that a  complete determination  of the
controversy may be had.
- _State v. Superior Court for King County_ (1912) 67 Wash. 355, 121 P. 847.

* Assignments | Key Number Topics 38k121 | In Name of Assignee

Assignee of chose in action may sue in his own name.
- _State v. Superior Court for King Cnty._, 67 Wash. 355, 121 P. 847 (1912)

** Explanation for Rule

1 Waiving other questions raised by the parties, we are convinced that
the writ  should not issue,  notwithstanding this court  has uniformly
held that an assignee of an  account or chose in action could maintain
a suit in  his own name, although  such an assignment is  made for the
purpose of collection only, and the  assignee had no other interest in
the thing  assigned. *The sum of  our holdings is that  an assignee has
sufficient interest to maintain the  suit.*
- _Conway v. Co-operative Home Builders_, 117  Pac.  716;
- _Von  Tobel v.  Stetson  & Post Mill  Co._, 32 Wash.  683, 73 Pac.  788;
- _McDaniel v.  Pressler_, 3 Wash.  636, 29 Pac. 209;
- _Riddell v.  Prichard_, 12 Wash. 601, 41 Pac. 905; Barto v. Seattle & Int. Ry. Co., 28 Wash. 179, 68 Pac. 442.

- _State v. Superior Court for King Cnty._, 67 Wash. 355, 356, 121 P. 847, 847 (1912)
